# Debt Freedom Workflow

Step-by-step guide for paying off debt systematically.

## Prerequisites
- List of all debts (balance, interest rate, minimum payment)
- Stable income to cover minimum payments
- Small emergency fund ($1,000 minimum) before aggressive payoff

## Step 1: Debt Inventory (Day 1)

Create a complete list:

| Debt | Balance | Interest Rate | Min Payment | Type |
|------|---------|---------------|-------------|------|
| Credit card 1 | $X | X% | $X | High-interest |
| Student loan | $X | X% | $X | Medium-interest |
| Car loan | $X | X% | $X | Medium-interest |
| Mortgage | $X | X% | $X | Low-interest |

Categorize:
- **Toxic debt** (>20% APR): credit cards, payday loans - eliminate ASAP
- **Bad debt** (8-20% APR): personal loans, some car loans - pay down aggressively
- **Neutral debt** (4-8% APR): student loans, some car loans - balance payoff vs investing
- **Acceptable debt** (<4% APR): mortgage - usually keep and invest surplus instead

## Step 2: Choose Your Method (Day 1-2)

### Avalanche Method (mathematically optimal)
1. Make minimum payments on all debts
2. Put ALL extra money toward the highest interest rate debt
3. When that's paid off, roll payment into next highest rate
4. Repeat until debt-free

**Best for:** Disciplined people who want to minimize total interest paid.

### Snowball Method (psychologically effective)
1. Make minimum payments on all debts
2. Put ALL extra money toward the smallest balance debt
3. When that's paid off, roll payment into next smallest
4. Repeat until debt-free

**Best for:** People who need motivation from quick wins.

### Hybrid Approach
1. Pay off any toxic debt (>20%) first regardless of balance
2. Then switch to snowball for remaining debts

## Step 3: Find Extra Money (Day 2-7)

Calculate your debt payoff budget:
- Monthly income minus essential expenses minus minimum payments = extra payoff money
- Look for cuts: subscriptions, dining out, unused memberships
- Consider temporary income boost: sell unused items, overtime, side gig
- Target: at least $200-$500/month extra toward debt

## Step 4: Set Up Automation (Day 7-10)

1. Auto-pay minimum payments on ALL debts (never miss a payment)
2. Set up extra payment to your target debt (auto-transfer after payday)
3. Call lenders to ensure extra payments go to principal (not future payments)

## Step 5: Accelerators

### Negotiate Lower Rates
- Credit cards: call and ask for lower APR (success rate ~50-70%)
- Student loans: check refinancing options (but weigh loss of federal protections)

### US-Specific Options
- **Student loan forgiveness**: PSLF (Public Service Loan Forgiveness) after 120 qualifying payments
- **Income-driven repayment**: IBR, PAYE, SAVE plans cap payments at % of discretionary income
- **0% balance transfer**: transfer credit card debt to 0% intro APR card (watch transfer fee 3-5%)
- **Debt consolidation loan**: combine high-rate debts into one lower-rate loan

### Canada-Specific Options
- **Consumer proposal**: negotiate to pay less than full amount (affects credit for 3 years after completion)
- **Debt consolidation program**: credit counseling agencies negotiate lower rates
- **Student loan interest**: federal student loan interest is tax-deductible
- **Bankruptcy**: last resort, discharged after 9-21 months for first-time (affects credit 6-7 years)
- **Orderly Payment of Debts (Alberta)**: court-ordered 5% interest on all debts

### India-Specific Options
- **Credit card**: Transfer to personal loan (lower rate), negotiate with bank for settlement (impacts CIBIL)
- **Personal loan prepayment**: No prepayment penalty after 12 months typically, check loan agreement
- **Home loan prepayment**: Zero penalty on floating rate (RBI mandate) — prepay aggressively if rate >8%
- **Education loan**: Section 80E interest deduction (no cap), for 8 years from start of repayment — keep this loan last due to tax benefit
- **Gold loan**: Cheapest secured loan option (7-10%), use for short-term needs instead of personal loan
- **Loan against FD/PPF/insurance**: 1-2% above FD rate — use before taking personal loan
- **Debt restructuring**: One-time settlement (OTS) possible but drops CIBIL score 75-100 points for 7 years
- **IBC/NCLT**: Insolvency route exists but mainly for corporates, personal insolvency framework still developing
- **Debt priority in India**: Credit cards (36%+) → personal loans (14-24%) → car loan (8-12%) → education loan (keep for 80E) → home loan (keep for 24(b) + 80C benefits, lowest rate)

## Step 6: Track Progress

### Monthly Check-in
- [ ] All minimum payments made on time
- [ ] Extra payment applied to target debt
- [ ] Updated debt balances
- [ ] Calculated total debt remaining
- [ ] Celebrated milestones (each debt paid off)

### Milestones
- [ ] Debt inventory complete
- [ ] Method chosen and automated
- [ ] First debt paid off (🎉 snowball win)
- [ ] All toxic debt (>20%) eliminated
- [ ] All bad debt (>8%) eliminated
- [ ] Completely debt-free (except mortgage)

## When to Invest Instead of Pay Off Debt

- Debt interest rate < expected investment return (roughly 5-7%)
- Always capture employer 401(k)/RRSP match first (guaranteed 50-100% return)
- Mortgage at 3-4% → likely better to invest surplus in index funds
- Credit card at 20% → always pay off first (no investment beats 20% guaranteed)

## Post-Debt Plan

Once debt-free:
1. Build emergency fund to 3-6 months expenses
2. Redirect all former debt payments to investing
3. Never carry credit card balance again
4. Use debt payoff amount as baseline for investment contributions
