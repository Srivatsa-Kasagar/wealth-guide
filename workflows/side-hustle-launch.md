# Side Hustle Launch Workflow

Step-by-step guide for starting a side business alongside your day job.

## Prerequisites
- At least 5-10 hours/week available
- Skill or interest to monetize
- $0-$500 startup budget (lean start)

## Step 1: Choose Your Hustle (Week 1)

### Decision Framework
Answer these questions:
1. What skills do I have that others would pay for?
2. How many hours per week can I commit? (be realistic)
3. How soon do I need income? (freelancing = fast, products = slow)
4. What's my startup budget?

### Quick-Start Categories (by speed to revenue)

| Category | Time to First $ | Startup Cost | $/Hour Potential |
|----------|----------------|--------------|-----------------|
| Freelancing (existing skills) | 1-4 weeks | $0 | $25-$200 |
| Service business | 1-2 weeks | $0-$200 | $25-$75 |
| Tutoring/coaching | 1-2 weeks | $0 | $30-$100 |
| Content creation | 3-12 months | $0-$500 | Highly variable |
| Digital products | 1-3 months | $0-$200 | Highly variable |
| E-commerce | 1-3 months | $200-$5,000 | Highly variable |

**Recommended first hustle:** Freelance or consult using skills from your day job. Fastest path to revenue with highest $/hour.

## Step 2: Set Up Your Business (Week 1-2)

### Minimum Viable Setup
1. Decide on a business name (or use your own name)
2. Create a simple portfolio/profile:
   - Freelancing: Upwork, Fiverr, or LinkedIn profile
   - Services: Google Business Profile, Nextdoor, local Facebook groups
   - Digital: Landing page (Carrd.co - free, or simple WordPress site)
3. Set up a separate bank account for business income
4. Start a simple expense tracker (spreadsheet is fine)

### US Business Setup
- **Default structure**: Sole proprietorship (no registration needed in most states)
- **Optional**: Form an LLC for liability protection ($50-$500 depending on state)
- **EIN**: Get a free EIN from irs.gov (useful for business banking, not always required for sole props)
- **Business license**: Check city/county requirements

### Canada Business Setup
- **Default structure**: Sole proprietorship
- **Business number (BN)**: Register with CRA if you need to collect GST/HST or hire employees
- **Provincial registration**: Some provinces require business name registration ($60-$100)
- **GST/HST**: Not required until $30,000 revenue in 4 consecutive quarters (but voluntary registration lets you claim input tax credits)

## Step 3: Get Your First Client (Week 2-4)

### Outreach Strategy
1. **Warm network first**: Tell friends, family, former colleagues what you're offering
2. **Platform applications**: Apply to 5-10 relevant jobs on Upwork/Fiverr per week
3. **Social proof**: Do 1-2 projects at reduced rate to get testimonials
4. **Content marketing**: Share expertise on LinkedIn or relevant communities

### Pricing
- **Research market rates** for your service (check platforms, ask in communities)
- **Start at 70-80% of market rate** to build portfolio, raise to market rate after 3-5 clients
- **Never work for free** (even discounted is better than free for perceived value)
- **Project-based > hourly** when possible (rewards efficiency)

## Step 4: Deliver and Get Paid (Ongoing)

### Invoicing
- Use free tools: Wave (accounting + invoicing), PayPal invoicing, Square invoices
- Set payment terms: due on receipt or net 15 for repeat clients
- Always get a deposit (25-50%) for larger projects

### Client Management
- Set clear scope and deliverables upfront
- Communicate proactively (updates, timelines)
- Over-deliver on first few projects (build reputation)
- Ask for testimonials and referrals after every good project

## Step 5: Handle Taxes (Quarterly)

### US Tax Obligations
1. Track all income and expenses (separate bank account helps)
2. Set aside 25-30% of net profit for taxes
3. Pay quarterly estimated taxes (April 15, June 15, Sep 15, Jan 15)
   - Form 1040-ES, pay via IRS Direct Pay
4. Deduct business expenses: home office, equipment, software, mileage, internet
5. File Schedule C with annual tax return
6. Pay self-employment tax: 15.3% on net profit

### Canada Tax Obligations
1. Track all income and expenses
2. Set aside 25-35% of net profit for taxes (income tax + CPP)
3. Pay quarterly installments if tax owing > $3,000
4. Deduct business expenses: home office (by square footage), supplies, vehicle, phone
5. Report on T2125 with annual tax return
6. CPP self-employed: ~11.9% on net income (both portions)
7. Register for GST/HST when revenue hits $30,000

## Step 6: Scale (Month 3+)

### Growth Milestones
- **$500/month**: You have a real side hustle. Optimize pricing and client acquisition.
- **$2,000/month**: Consider raising rates 20%. You're undercharging if fully booked.
- **$4,000/month**: Evaluate: scale further, maintain, or transition to full-time?
- **Matching day job income**: Serious decision point about going full-time.

### Scaling Strategies
1. Raise prices (easiest lever)
2. Productize your service (packages instead of custom quotes)
3. Outsource/delegate low-value tasks
4. Build systems (templates, processes, automation)
5. Add passive revenue streams (courses, templates, digital products)
