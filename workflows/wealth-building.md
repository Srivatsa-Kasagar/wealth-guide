# Wealth Building Workflow

Long-term wealth accumulation strategy for someone with a financial foundation in place.

## Prerequisites
- Emergency fund: 3-6 months expenses
- No high-interest debt (>8% APR)
- Stable income with monthly surplus
- Basic investment account(s) opened

## Phase 1: Optimize Tax-Advantaged Accounts (Month 1-3)

### US Account Ladder
Max contributions in this order:

| Priority | Account | Annual Limit | Tax Benefit |
|----------|---------|-------------|-------------|
| 1 | 401(k) employer match | Up to match % | 50-100% instant return |
| 2 | HSA (if eligible) | $4,300/$8,550 | Triple tax advantage |
| 3 | Roth IRA | $7,000 | Tax-free growth forever |
| 4 | Remaining 401(k) | $23,500 total | Tax-deferred growth |
| 5 | Mega Backdoor Roth | Up to $70,000 total | Tax-free growth |
| 6 | Taxable brokerage | Unlimited | Tax-efficient placement |

### Canada Account Ladder
Max contributions in this order:

| Priority | Account | Annual Limit | Tax Benefit |
|----------|---------|-------------|-------------|
| 1 | Employer RRSP match | Up to match % | 50-100% instant return |
| 2 | TFSA | $7,000 | Tax-free growth and withdrawals |
| 3 | RRSP | $31,560 (or 18% of income) | Tax-deductible, tax-deferred |
| 4 | FHSA (if eligible) | $8,000 | Deductible + tax-free for home |
| 5 | RESP (if children) | $2,500/year for 20% CESG | Tax-deferred, grants |
| 6 | Non-registered | Unlimited | Capital gains + dividend credits |

### Action Items
- [ ] Calculate how much you can contribute monthly to max accounts
- [ ] Set up automatic contributions aligned with payday
- [ ] Increase contributions with every raise (at least 50% of raise goes to investing)

## Phase 2: Build Your Investment Portfolio (Month 2-6)

### Asset Allocation by Risk Tolerance

| Risk Level | Stocks | Bonds | Alternatives |
|------------|--------|-------|-------------|
| Conservative | 40-60% | 30-50% | 0-10% |
| Moderate | 60-80% | 15-30% | 0-10% |
| Aggressive | 80-100% | 0-15% | 0-10% |

### Age-Based Rule of Thumb
- Stocks: 110 minus your age (e.g., age 30 = 80% stocks)
- Adjust based on risk tolerance, income stability, and goals

### Tax-Efficient Placement

| Account Type | Best Assets |
|-------------|-------------|
| Tax-deferred (401k/RRSP) | Bonds, REITs (high-tax distributions) |
| Tax-free (Roth/TFSA) | Highest-growth assets (stocks, small-cap) |
| Taxable | US index funds (low turnover), tax-efficient ETFs |

### US Portfolio Examples
- **Simple**: 100% VT (Total World Stock) or target-date fund
- **Three-fund**: 60% VTI + 30% VXUS + 10% BND
- **Income-focused**: Add 10% VNQ (REIT) + 10% SCHD (dividend)

### Canada Portfolio Examples
- **Simple**: 100% VEQT or VGRO (all-in-one)
- **Three-fund**: 30% VCN + 45% XAW + 25% ZAG
- **Income-focused**: Add 10% ZRE (REIT) + reduce bonds accordingly

## Phase 3: Optimize and Grow (Month 6-12)

### Increase Income
1. Negotiate salary (aim for 10%+ at next review or job switch)
2. Pursue high-ROI certifications or skills
3. Start or grow a side income stream
4. Rule: invest at least 50% of every income increase

### Reduce Tax Drag
- US: tax-loss harvest in taxable accounts annually
- US: maximize Roth contributions (tax-free growth compounds)
- Canada: optimize RRSP vs TFSA based on current vs expected retirement tax bracket
- Canada: use eligible Canadian dividends in non-registered accounts (dividend tax credit)
- India: Equity mutual fund dividends taxed at slab rate, but LTCG on equity held >1 year taxed at only 12.5% above ₹1.25L — prefer growth option over dividend option for tax efficiency
- India: Maximize Section 80C (PPF/ELSS), NPS 80CCD(1B) extra ₹50K, health insurance 80D before investing in taxable accounts. SGBs offer 2.5% interest + zero LTCG if held to maturity — best gold vehicle.
- Both: avoid frequent trading (capital gains taxes)

### Automate Everything
1. Automatic payroll deductions to retirement accounts
2. Automatic transfers to brokerage after payday
3. Automatic investment purchases (most brokerages support this)
4. Automatic rebalancing (target-date funds, robo-advisors, or annual manual)

## Phase 4: Wealth Acceleration (Year 2+)

### Net Worth Milestones

| Milestone | Significance |
|-----------|-------------|
| $0 (net worth positive) | Out of debt - foundation set |
| $100,000 | Hardest milestone - compound interest starts to matter |
| $250,000 | Investment returns become meaningful vs contributions |
| $500,000 | Coast FI possibility (could stop contributing and still retire on time) |
| $1,000,000 | Investment returns likely exceed annual salary contributions |
| 25x expenses | Financial independence achieved |

### Diversification Beyond Stocks and Bonds
Consider adding (only after core portfolio is solid):
- Real estate: rental property or REITs
- I-Bonds/TIPS (US) or real-return bonds (Canada): inflation protection
- Alternative investments: only with money you can afford to lose

### Protect Your Wealth
- Insurance: life (if dependents), disability, umbrella liability
- Estate planning: will, beneficiary designations, power of attorney
- US: consider trust structure at higher net worth
- Canada: beneficiary designations on TFSA/RRSP bypass probate

## Annual Review Checklist
- [ ] Rebalance portfolio (if >5% off target allocation)
- [ ] Max out tax-advantaged accounts
- [ ] Review insurance coverage
- [ ] Update beneficiary designations
- [ ] Tax-loss harvest (November-December)
- [ ] Review and increase savings rate
- [ ] Check net worth progress against milestones
- [ ] Reassess risk tolerance and time horizon
