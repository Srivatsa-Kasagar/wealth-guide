# Career & Income Growth

Strategies for growing primary income through career development, negotiation, and equity compensation.

## Salary Negotiation

### Research Your Market Value
- **Glassdoor:** Salary reports by company, role, location
- **Levels.fyi:** Tech compensation data (base + equity + bonus breakdown)
- **Payscale:** Salary data across industries
- **LinkedIn Salary Insights:** Data from LinkedIn profiles
- **Robert Half Salary Guide:** Professional services, finance, accounting
- Compare: role, location, experience level, company size, industry

### Negotiation Framework
1. **Anchor high:** State a specific number 10-20% above your target
2. **Justify with data:** "Based on market data for this role in this market, the range is X-Y"
3. **Emphasize value:** Quantify your impact (revenue generated, costs saved, projects delivered)
4. **Use silence:** After stating your number, stop talking. Let them respond.
5. **Never accept immediately:** "Thank you, I'd like to take a day to review this."

### Timing
- **Best time:** After receiving an offer (most leverage), during annual review with documented achievements
- **After major accomplishment:** Completed big project, landed major client, exceeded targets
- **Market timing:** When your skills are in high demand or company is growing fast
- **Worst time:** During layoffs, poor company performance, right after starting

### Beyond Base Salary
- Signing bonus: $5,000-$50,000+ (one-time, easier for companies to approve)
- Equity/RSUs: Can significantly increase total compensation
- Remote work: Saves $5,000-$15,000/year in commuting, food, clothing
- Extra PTO: 1-2 weeks additional vacation
- Professional development budget: $2,000-$10,000/year
- Title upgrade: May not cost company anything but helps future negotiations

### Common Mistakes
- Accepting the first offer without negotiating (money left on table)
- Giving a range instead of specific number (they'll pick the low end)
- Anchoring too low based on current salary
- Focusing only on base salary (total comp = base + equity + bonus + benefits)
- Not practicing the conversation beforehand
- Negotiating over email when a call would be more effective

## Career Capital & Skill Stacking

### Career Capital (Cal Newport)
- Rare and valuable skills command premium compensation
- Build expertise through deliberate practice, not just years of experience
- "So Good They Can't Ignore You" - skills trump passion for career satisfaction
- Focus: what skills does the market value that I can develop?

### Skill Stacking (Scott Adams)
- Being top 1% in one skill is extremely hard
- Being top 25% in 2-3 complementary skills is achievable and creates unique value
- **Examples:**
  - Coding + finance + communication = fintech product manager
  - Design + marketing + data = growth designer
  - Writing + domain expertise + SEO = high-value content consultant
  - Engineering + leadership + public speaking = VP of Engineering

### High-ROI Skills for Income Growth
1. **Public speaking / presentation:** Differentiates at every level
2. **Data analysis:** Valued across all industries
3. **Project management:** Enables leadership roles
4. **Sales / persuasion:** Directly tied to revenue
5. **Writing:** Clear communication is rare and valuable
6. **Leadership / people management:** Required for senior roles
7. **Financial literacy:** Better decisions at business and personal level

### T-Shaped Professional
- **Vertical bar:** Deep expertise in one area (your primary skill)
- **Horizontal bar:** Broad knowledge across adjacent areas
- More valuable than pure specialists or pure generalists
- Build depth first (years 0-5), then breadth (years 5+)

## Job Hopping vs Staying

### The Data
- **Average raise (staying):** 3-5% annually
- **Average raise (switching):** 10-20% or more
- **Loyalty penalty:** Staying at one company for 5+ years often means being 15-30% underpaid vs market

### When to Switch
- Learning has plateaued (no new skills for 6+ months)
- Below-market compensation (>10% under comparable roles)
- No clear promotion path or growth opportunities
- Toxic culture or poor management
- Better opportunity aligned with long-term goals

### When to Stay
- Active mentorship from strong leaders
- Equity vesting on meaningful schedule (RSUs, options)
- Clear promotion track with timeline
- Rare learning opportunity (new technology, market, leadership chance)
- Work-life balance that matches your priorities
- Internal mobility option (new team/role without switching companies)

### Optimal Tenure
- **2-4 years per role** generally optimal for career growth
- Less than 2 years repeatedly: "job hopper" perception (less concern in tech)
- More than 5 years: risk of being pigeonholed, falling behind market comp
- Industry-dependent: government/academia value tenure, tech/startup value growth

### Internal Mobility
- Switching teams or roles within company captures some benefit of job switching
- Maintains seniority, benefits, equity vesting
- Often easier to negotiate than external moves
- Builds broader organizational knowledge

## Equity Compensation

### RSUs (Restricted Stock Units)

**How they work:** Company grants X shares, they vest over time (typically 4 years with 1-year cliff).

**US Tax Treatment:**
- Taxed as ordinary income on vest date (shares x price on vest = W-2 income)
- Employer withholds taxes (often sells shares to cover)
- Subsequent gains/losses: capital gains/losses from vest-date price
- Long-term rate if held >1 year after vesting

**Canada Tax Treatment:**
- Taxed as employment income on vest date (full value added to T4)
- 50% stock option deduction may NOT apply to RSUs (it applies to qualifying stock options)
- Subsequent gains: capital gains from vest-date cost basis
- Employer remits withholding tax

**Strategy:**
- Default: sell on vest, diversify (don't hold concentrated company stock position)
- Rule of thumb: hold no more than 10-15% of net worth in employer stock
- Tax-loss harvesting opportunity if price drops after vesting

### Stock Options (ISOs vs NSOs)

**ISOs (Incentive Stock Options) - US Only:**
- No regular tax on exercise (but AMT may apply on spread)
- If held 1 year after exercise + 2 years after grant: long-term capital gains on entire spread
- If sold earlier (disqualifying disposition): spread taxed as ordinary income
- AMT risk: large exercises can trigger Alternative Minimum Tax

**NSOs (Non-Qualified Stock Options):**
- Spread (market price - exercise price) taxed as ordinary income at exercise
- Subsequent gains taxed as capital gains
- Available to employees and contractors
- No AMT complications

**Canada Stock Options:**
- If granted by CCPC (Canadian-Controlled Private Corporation): tax deferred until shares sold
- Non-CCPC: taxed on exercise (employment income)
- 50% stock option deduction available if: exercise price >= FMV at grant, shares are prescribed, and other conditions met
- Effectively: 50% of option benefit taxed at marginal rate

### ESPP (Employee Stock Purchase Plan)

**How it works:**
- Contribute 1-15% of salary during 6-month offering period
- Purchase stock at 15% discount from lower of start or end price
- "Look-back" provision can increase effective discount significantly

**Tax (US):**
- Qualifying disposition (held 2 years from offering + 1 year from purchase): discount taxed as ordinary income, remainder as capital gains
- Disqualifying disposition (sold earlier): discount taxed as ordinary income

**Strategy:**
- Max contribution, sell immediately on purchase date = guaranteed ~15% return (minus short-term holding)
- Low risk strategy: automatic payroll deduction -> discounted purchase -> immediate sale -> reinvest in diversified portfolio

### Key Equity Decisions
- **How much company stock to hold:** 10-15% of net worth maximum (concentration risk)
- **When to sell RSUs:** Usually on vest (diversification), unless specific tax optimization reason
- **Options exercise timing:** Balance tax impact vs risk of holding concentrated position
- **ESPP:** Almost always worth participating (guaranteed discount)

## Income Growth Trajectory

### By Career Stage
- **Early career (0-5 years):** Fastest growth period. Job hop strategically (every 2-3 years). Build foundational skills. Target: 10-15% annual income growth.
- **Mid career (5-15 years):** Specialize deeply OR move into management. Negotiate aggressively based on track record. Target: 8-12% annual growth.
- **Senior (15+ years):** Leverage expertise for consulting, advisory roles. Income from knowledge (speaking, courses, books). Target: 5-10% growth (base may plateau but total comp grows via equity, consulting).

### Rules of Thumb
- Aim for 10%+ annual income growth in first 10 years
- Invest at least 50% of every raise (avoid lifestyle inflation)
- Your career is your biggest asset early on (investing in skills has highest ROI)
- Network compounds like money does (invest in relationships)

## Multiple Income Streams

### Progression
1. **Optimize salary first** - usually the biggest and most efficient lever
2. **Start a side hustle** - leverage career skills for consulting/freelancing
3. **Build investment income** - dividends, interest, rental income
4. **Create passive income** - digital products, royalties, licensing

### Reality Check
- "Passive income" requires months/years of active effort to build
- Most millionaires have 3+ income streams (but they built them sequentially, not simultaneously)
- Don't spread too thin - master one stream before adding another
- Salary + investing + one side income is a solid foundation for most people
