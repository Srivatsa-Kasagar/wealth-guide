# Side Hustles

Side income strategies, platforms, and tax implications for US and Canadian earners.

## Freelancing / Consulting

### High-Value Skills
- Software development: $50-$200+/hour
- UX/UI design: $40-$150/hour
- Copywriting / content writing: $30-$100/hour
- Data analysis / visualization: $40-$120/hour
- Bookkeeping / accounting: $25-$75/hour
- Digital marketing / SEO: $30-$100/hour
- Video editing: $25-$75/hour

### Platforms
- **Upwork:** Largest marketplace, competitive, good for building portfolio
- **Fiverr:** Fixed-price gigs, good for productized services
- **Toptal:** Premium network, rigorous screening, top 3% freelancers, high rates
- **LinkedIn ProFinder:** Professional services, B2B focus
- **Contra:** Commission-free, portfolio-focused

### Pricing Strategy
- **Hourly:** Simple, transparent, clients prefer for ongoing work. Risk: trading time for money.
- **Project-based:** Better margins, rewards efficiency. Scope clearly to avoid scope creep.
- **Value-based:** Price based on value delivered, not time spent. Highest margins. Requires experience to estimate and sell.
- **Starting rate:** 70-80% of market rate to build portfolio, raise after 3-5 successful projects.

### Time Commitment
- 5-10 hours/week: 1-2 small projects, $500-$2,000/month
- 10-20 hours/week: 2-3 projects, $2,000-$5,000/month
- 20+ hours/week: approaching part-time business territory

## E-Commerce & Digital Products

### Dropshipping
- Sell products without holding inventory (supplier ships directly)
- Platforms: Shopify + Oberlo/DSers, WooCommerce
- Startup cost: $100-$500 (store setup, initial ads)
- Margins: 15-30% typical, competitive
- Risk: quality control, shipping times, customer service burden

### Print-on-Demand
- Custom designs on products (t-shirts, mugs, phone cases)
- Platforms: Printful, Redbubble, Merch by Amazon, TeeSpring
- Startup cost: $0-$100 (design tools)
- Margins: 10-30% depending on platform and pricing
- Good creative outlet, low risk

### Digital Products
- **Online courses:** Teachable, Udemy, Kajabi. Revenue: $0-$50,000+/month for successful courses.
- **Ebooks:** Amazon KDP, Gumroad. Revenue: $100-$5,000/month.
- **Templates:** Notion, Canva, Excel templates. Revenue: $100-$2,000/month.
- **Software/SaaS:** Highest earning potential, highest skill/time requirement.
- Startup cost: $0-$500 (time is the main investment)
- Key advantage: create once, sell many times (scalable)

### E-Commerce Platforms
- **Etsy:** Handmade, vintage, digital downloads. Fees: 6.5% transaction + $0.20 listing.
- **Amazon FBA:** Higher capital requirement ($2K-$10K), logistics handled by Amazon, massive audience.
- **Shopify:** Own store, full control, $39/month + transaction fees.

## Content Creation & Monetization

### YouTube
- Ad revenue: $3-$5 RPM (revenue per 1,000 views) typical, varies by niche
- Monetization requirements: 1,000 subscribers + 4,000 watch hours
- Additional revenue: sponsorships ($500-$50,000+ per video depending on audience), affiliate marketing, merchandise
- Timeline: 6-18 months to monetization for consistent creators
- Highest-earning niches: finance, tech, business, education

### Newsletter / Blog
- Platforms: Substack (free, takes 10% of paid), Ghost (self-hosted), WordPress
- Revenue: paid subscriptions, sponsorships, affiliate marketing
- Growth: consistent publishing + social media + SEO
- Timeline: 6-12 months to meaningful revenue
- Key metric: email list size (1,000+ subscribers for sponsorship viability)

### Podcasting
- Revenue: sponsorships ($15-$50 CPM), premium content, affiliate marketing
- Lower barrier to start than video but harder to monetize
- Equipment: $100-$500 for decent setup

### Social Media
- Instagram/TikTok: brand deals, affiliate marketing
- Revenue varies dramatically (power law: top 1% earn most)
- Focus on niche expertise, not general lifestyle content

## US Tax Implications

### Reporting
- All side income reported on **Schedule C** (sole proprietorship is default)
- Net profit = gross income - business expenses
- Net profit flows to Form 1040 and is subject to income tax + self-employment tax

### Self-Employment Tax
- **15.3%** of net profit (12.4% Social Security + 2.9% Medicare)
- Applies in addition to regular income tax
- Can deduct 50% of SE tax on Form 1040 (above-the-line deduction)
- Social Security portion caps at $176,100 (2025)
- Additional 0.9% Medicare tax on earnings above $200K single / $250K married

### Quarterly Estimated Taxes
- Required if you expect to owe $1,000+ in tax for the year
- Due dates: April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15
- Underpayment penalty if you don't pay enough throughout the year
- Safe harbor: pay 100% of prior year tax (110% if AGI > $150K)
- Pay via IRS Direct Pay or EFTPS

### QBI Deduction (Section 199A)
- **20% deduction** on qualified business income for pass-through entities (sole proprietors, LLCs, S-corps)
- Phases out above $197,300 single / $394,600 married (2025) for specified service businesses
- **Expires after 2025** if TCJA is not extended

### Common Deductions
- Home office: $5/sq ft simplified method (max $1,500) or actual expenses (pro-rated by office %)
- Equipment and software (Section 179 immediate deduction)
- Vehicle: $0.70/mile (2025) or actual expenses
- Internet and phone (business use percentage)
- Professional development, courses, books
- Health insurance premiums (if self-employed and no employer plan available)
- Retirement contributions: SEP IRA (25% of net income, max $70,000), Solo 401(k) ($23,500 employee + 25% employer)

### Business Structure
- **Sole proprietorship:** Default, no setup needed. Personal liability.
- **LLC:** Liability protection, $50-$500 to form (varies by state). Same tax treatment as sole prop by default.
- **S-Corp election:** When net income > ~$40K-$60K. Pay yourself "reasonable salary" + take remaining as distributions (saves self-employment tax on distribution portion). Requires payroll setup.

## Canada Tax Implications

### Reporting
- Report on **T2125** (Statement of Business Activities) with annual tax return
- Net business income added to total income, taxed at marginal rate
- Keep all receipts and records for 6 years

### CPP Self-Employed Contributions
- Self-employed pay both employee and employer portions
- Combined rate (CPP1): ~11.9% of net self-employment income (2025)
- Applies on income between $3,500 and $71,300 (first ceiling)
- CPP2: Additional 8% on earnings between $71,300 and $81,200 (second ceiling)
- Maximum self-employed total (CPP1+CPP2): ~$8,860

### GST/HST
- **Registration mandatory** when revenue exceeds $30,000 in 4 consecutive quarters
- **Voluntary registration** possible at any revenue level (lets you claim input tax credits on business expenses)
- Must charge GST/HST on services/products and remit to CRA
- File GST/HST return annually (quarterly or monthly if over $1.5M)

### Common Deductions
- Home office: calculate by square footage as % of total home
- Supplies, equipment, software
- Vehicle: keep a mileage log, deduct business-use percentage of expenses
- Phone and internet (business-use percentage)
- Professional development and courses
- Meals with clients (50% deductible)

### Business Structure
- **Sole proprietorship:** Default, minimal registration. Report income on personal return.
- **Incorporation:** Consider when net business income consistently > $50K-$80K. Small business tax rate ~11-12.5% (combined). Allows income deferral, income splitting, and liability protection.
- Provincial business registration may be required (varies by province, $60-$100 typical)

## AI-Enhanced Side Hustles (2024+)
- **AI-assisted freelancing:** Use AI tools to 2-5x productivity in writing, design, coding, marketing. Higher effective $/hour.
- **AI consulting:** Help small businesses implement AI tools and workflows ($75-$200/hour)
- **Prompt engineering / AI training:** Data labeling, RLHF work for AI companies. Platforms: Outlier, Scale AI, Surge AI.
- **AI-generated content:** Course creation, ebook writing, template design accelerated by AI tools
- **Key principle:** AI is a productivity multiplier for existing skills, not a replacement for expertise. The highest-value AI side hustles combine domain expertise + AI tooling.

## Getting Started Framework

1. **Assess** (Week 1): List your skills, available hours (5-20/week), and financial goals
2. **Choose** (Week 1): Pick the hustle that maximizes skill-based $/hour
3. **Setup** (Week 2): Create profiles/portfolio, business bank account, expense tracker
4. **Launch** (Week 2-3): Start outreach, apply to platforms, tell your network
5. **First revenue** (Week 3-6): Land first paying client/customer
6. **Milestone** (Month 2-3): Hit $500/month consistently
7. **Optimize** (Month 3+): Raise prices, productize, build systems
8. **Scale or maintain** (Month 6+): Decide whether to grow or keep as side income
