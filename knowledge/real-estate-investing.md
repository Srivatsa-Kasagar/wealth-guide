# Real Estate Investing

Primary residence, rental properties, and REITs for US, Canadian, and Indian investors.

## Primary Residence

### Rent vs Buy Analysis
- **Price-to-rent ratio:** Annual rent / home price. Below 15 = buying favored, 15-20 = neutral, above 20 = renting favored
- **Break-even timeline:** Typically 5-7 years to break even on buying costs (closing costs, moving, maintenance)
- **True cost of ownership:** Mortgage + property tax + insurance + maintenance (1-2% of value/year) + HOA + opportunity cost of down payment
- Primary home is shelter first, investment second

### Mortgage Types
- **Fixed rate:** Predictable payments, protection against rate increases
- **Variable/ARM:** Lower initial rate, risk of rate increases
- **15-year vs 30-year (US):** 15yr saves massive interest but higher monthly payment
- **5-year fixed (Canada):** Most common Canadian term, renewed every 5 years

### Down Payment
- **20% down:** Avoids mortgage insurance, better rates, lower monthly payment
- **Less than 20%:** Possible with insurance (PMI in US, CMHC in Canada)
- **Saving strategy:** Automate savings, consider high-yield account, use tax-advantaged accounts (FHSA/HBP in Canada, FHA programs in US)

## US Home Buying

### Loan Programs

| Program | Min Down | Credit Score | Notes |
|---------|----------|-------------|-------|
| Conventional | 3-5% | 620+ | PMI until 80% LTV |
| FHA | 3.5% | 580+ | MIP for life of loan (if <10% down) |
| VA | 0% | No minimum | Veterans/active military, no PMI |
| USDA | 0% | 640+ | Rural areas, income limits |

- **PMI (Private Mortgage Insurance):** 0.5-1% of loan annually, removed at 80% LTV (request) or 78% LTV (automatic)
- **Down payment assistance:** State and local programs, employer programs, grants for first-time buyers

### US Tax Benefits
- **Mortgage interest deduction:** Deductible on loans up to $750,000 (must itemize)
- **Property tax deduction:** Deductible up to $10,000 combined SALT cap
- **Capital gains exclusion:** $250,000 single / $500,000 married on primary residence sale (must live in 2 of last 5 years)

## Canada Home Buying

### First-Time Buyer Programs
- **FHSA:** $8,000/year, $40,000 lifetime. Tax-deductible + tax-free withdrawal for qualifying home purchase.
- **RRSP Home Buyers' Plan (HBP):** Withdraw up to $60,000 from RRSP. Must repay over 15 years (or withdrawal counted as income).
- **First-Time Home Buyer Incentive:** Government shared equity (5-10% of home price). Must repay on sale or after 25 years.
- **First-Time Home Buyer Tax Credit:** $10,000 non-refundable credit ($1,500 tax reduction)
- **GST/HST New Housing Rebate:** Partial rebate on new homes under $450,000

### Canadian Mortgage Rules
- **Stress test:** Must qualify at contract rate + 2% or 5.25% (whichever is higher)
- **CMHC insurance:** Required if down payment < 20%. Premium: 2.8-4.0% of mortgage, added to loan.
- **Maximum amortization:** 25 years for insured mortgages, up to 30 years for uninsured
- **Land transfer tax:** Varies by province (0% in Alberta/Saskatchewan/rural areas, up to 2%+ in Ontario/BC). First-time buyer rebates available in some provinces.

### Canadian Tax Treatment
- **No mortgage interest deduction** (unlike US)
- **Principal residence exemption:** No capital gains tax on primary home sale (must designate)
- **Smith Manoeuvre (advanced, controversial):** Borrow against home equity to invest, making mortgage interest tax-deductible. This is a leveraged strategy with significant risk — amplifies both gains AND losses. Requires readvanceable mortgage, meticulous CRA documentation, and professional advice. NOT suitable for most homeowners. Only consider if: (1) already maxed RRSP/TFSA, (2) stable income with high risk tolerance, (3) long time horizon, (4) working with an accountant experienced in this strategy. Many financial advisors do not recommend it.

## India Real Estate

### Home Buying in India

#### Costs Beyond Purchase Price
- **Stamp duty:** 5-8% (varies by state — Maharashtra 6%, Karnataka 5%, Delhi 6%)
- **Registration charges:** 1-2%
- **GST:** 5% on under-construction (1% affordable housing <₹45L), 0% on ready-to-move
- **Legal fees, brokerage:** 1-2%
- **Total transaction cost:** ~8-12% of property value

#### Home Loan Structure
- **LTV:** Up to 90% for loans up to ₹30L, 80% for ₹30-₹75L, 75% above ₹75L
- **Tenure:** Up to 30 years (most opt for 15-20)
- **Rates:** Floating ~8.5-9.5% (linked to repo rate), Fixed rare and higher
- **EMI rule:** Keep under 40% of net monthly income
- **Prepayment:** No penalty on floating rate loans (RBI mandate)
- **Processing fee:** 0.5-1% of loan amount

#### RERA (Real Estate Regulation Act)
- Mandatory registration of projects with state RERA
- Builder must deposit 70% of buyer funds in escrow
- Carpet area disclosure mandatory (not super built-up)
- Penalties for delays in possession
- Always verify RERA registration before buying

#### Tax Benefits on Home Loan (Old Regime)
- **Principal:** Section 80C up to ₹1.5L (within overall 80C limit)
- **Interest (self-occupied):** Section 24(b) up to ₹2L/year
- **Interest (let-out):** No limit, but loss from house property set-off capped at ₹2L
- **First-time buyer:** Section 80EEA additional ₹1.5L interest (check current availability)
- **Pre-construction interest:** Deductible in 5 equal installments after possession

#### Rent vs Buy in India
- **Rental yields in Indian metros:** 2-3% (very low vs 5-7% globally)
- **Price-to-rent ratio:** 30-50x in metros (signals overvaluation for investment)
- Often cheaper to rent and invest the difference in equity
- **Buy makes sense:** If staying 10+ years, emotional/family reasons, or if HRA benefit is not available
- **Don't buy as primary "investment":** Indian real estate appreciation has underperformed equity over last decade in most cities

### Real Estate as Investment in India

#### Rental Property
- Rental income taxable at slab rate (30% deduction for repairs/maintenance)
- Municipal taxes deductible
- **TDS:** Tenant must deduct 10% TDS if annual rent exceeds ₹2.4L (for individuals paying to residents)
- Vacancy risk and tenant protection laws vary by state (some very tenant-friendly)

#### Capital Gains on Property
- **LTCG (held >2 years):** 12.5% without indexation (post July 2024 budget)
- **STCG (held ≤2 years):** At slab rate
- **Section 54:** Reinvest LTCG in 1 residential property within 2 years (purchase) or 3 years (construction) to exempt gains
- **Section 54EC:** Invest up to ₹50L in NHAI/REC bonds within 6 months (5-year lock-in)
- **Section 54F:** Sale of any capital asset (non-house), invest in residential house

#### REITs in India
- **Listed on exchanges:** Embassy REIT, Mindspace REIT, Brookfield India REIT, Nexus Select Trust
- **Minimum investment:** 1 unit (~₹300-400)
- **Dividend yield:** 5-7% (higher than direct property rental yield)
- 90% of net distributable cash flow paid to unitholders
- **Tax:** Dividends taxable at slab, capital gains like equity (listed)
- Better liquidity, diversification, and professional management vs direct property
- Good alternative for real estate exposure without large capital

#### Fractional Ownership Platforms
- Strata, hBits, PropertyShare — invest in commercial property from ₹10-25L
- Not SEBI-regulated as REITs (higher risk)
- Illiquid, lock-in periods
- Approach with caution — prefer listed REITs for most investors

### NRI Real Estate
- Can buy residential and commercial property (not agricultural land)
- Home loan available from Indian banks (higher rates, ~8.5-10%)
- **TDS:** 20% + surcharge on LTCG for NRI seller (buyer must deduct)
- **Repatriation rules:** Up to 2 properties, subject to FEMA compliance
- Power of Attorney common for NRI transactions
- Can claim home loan tax benefits if filing Indian tax returns

### Common Real Estate Mistakes in India
- Buying under-construction without RERA check
- Over-leveraging (EMI >50% of income)
- Treating primary residence as an investment
- Ignoring maintenance charges and society fees (₹3-15K/month in metros)
- Not accounting for stamp duty + registration in budget
- Buying in tier-3 cities for "appreciation" without rental demand
- Black money component (avoid — increasing scrutiny, harder to get loans)

## Rental Property Investing

### Cash Flow Analysis
```
Monthly rent
- Mortgage payment (principal + interest)
- Property taxes
- Insurance
- Maintenance (budget 1-2% of property value/year)
- Vacancy (budget 5-10% of annual rent)
- Property management (8-12% of rent, if using manager)
- CapEx reserve (5-10% of rent for major repairs)
= Net cash flow
```

### Key Metrics
- **Cap rate:** Net Operating Income / Purchase Price. Target: 5-10% depending on market.
- **Cash-on-cash return:** Annual cash flow / total cash invested. Target: 8-12%.
- **1% rule (rough screen):** Monthly rent should be >= 1% of purchase price. Hard to find in expensive markets.
- **50% rule (rough estimate):** About 50% of rent goes to expenses (excluding mortgage). Net operating income ~ 50% of gross rent.

### Leverage
- Mortgage allows you to control a large asset with a fraction of the cost
- Amplifies both gains AND losses
- Example: $200K property with $40K down. If property appreciates 5% ($10K), that's a 25% return on your $40K cash investment.
- Risk: negative cash flow, declining values, vacancy

### Property Management
- **Self-manage:** Save 8-12% of rent, but time-intensive (tenant screening, maintenance calls, legal compliance)
- **Hire manager:** 8-12% of monthly rent typical. Worth it for out-of-area properties or multiple units.

## US Rental Tax Benefits

### Depreciation
- **Residential:** 27.5-year straight-line depreciation on building value (not land)
- **Commercial:** 39 years
- "Phantom" expense that reduces taxable rental income without cash outflow
- **Depreciation recapture:** Taxed at 25% on sale (you give back the tax benefit)
- **Cost segregation:** Reclassify building components for accelerated depreciation (5, 7, 15 year). Worth it for properties above ~$500K.

### 1031 Exchange
- Defer capital gains by selling investment property and buying "like-kind" replacement
- Must identify replacement within 45 days, close within 180 days
- Use qualified intermediary (cannot touch the funds)
- Can compound wealth by repeatedly exchanging into larger properties
- **No 1031 on primary residence** (use capital gains exclusion instead)

### Passive Activity Rules
- Rental losses are "passive" and can only offset passive income
- **Exception:** If AGI < $100K, can deduct up to $25,000 in rental losses against other income
- Phases out between $100K-$150K AGI
- **Real estate professional status:** If 750+ hours/year in real estate, losses become non-passive (fully deductible)

## Canada Rental Tax Specifics

### Capital Cost Allowance (CCA)
- **Residential buildings:** 4% declining balance (Class 1)
- **Furniture/appliances:** 20% declining balance (Class 8)
- Cannot use CCA to create or increase a rental loss (can only reduce rental income to zero)
- **Recapture:** If you sell for more than undepreciated capital cost, recapture taxed as income

### No 1031 Equivalent
- Capital gains triggered on sale of investment property
- 50% inclusion rate on first $250K, 66.7% above
- **Reserve provision:** If seller financing, can spread gains over up to 5 years
- **Principal residence exemption** does NOT apply to rental properties

### Other Canadian Rules
- GST/HST on substantially renovated properties or new construction
- Short-term rental restrictions in many municipalities (Airbnb regulations)
- Non-resident withholding tax (25%) on rental income paid to non-residents
- Rental income reported on T776

## REITs (Real Estate Investment Trusts)

### What They Are
- Publicly traded companies that own and operate real estate
- Must distribute 90%+ of taxable income as dividends
- Provides real estate exposure without owning physical property

### Pros and Cons
**Pros:** Liquidity (buy/sell like stocks), diversification (many properties), low minimum investment, professional management, no tenant headaches
**Cons:** Correlated with stock market (especially short-term), REIT dividends taxed as ordinary income (not qualified dividends), no direct control, no leverage benefit

### Key REIT ETFs
- **US:** VNQ (Vanguard Real Estate ETF), SCHH (Schwab US REIT)
- **Canada:** ZRE (BMO Equal Weight REITs), XRE (iShares S&P/TSX Capped REIT)

### Tax-Efficient Placement
- REIT distributions taxed as ordinary income (highest rate)
- **Best held in tax-advantaged accounts** (401k/RRSP/TFSA) to shelter distributions
- In taxable accounts, consider total portfolio allocation vs tax drag

## Real Estate Crowdfunding

### Platforms
- **Fundrise:** Min $10, eREITs and eFunds, 5-12% average returns, ~1% annual fees, quarterly liquidity (with penalties)
- **CrowdStreet:** Accredited investors, individual deal selection, higher minimums ($25K+)
- **AcreTrader / FarmTogether:** Farmland investing, $10-$1,000 minimums, 8-10% target returns

### Pros and Cons
**Pros:** Lower minimums than direct ownership, diversification across properties, no property management, access to commercial real estate
**Cons:** Low liquidity (multi-year lock-ups), fees can be complex, less transparent than public REITs, platform risk, limited track record vs public markets

### When to Consider
- Already maxed out tax-advantaged accounts and have a diversified index portfolio
- Want real estate exposure beyond REITs without direct ownership hassles
- Comfortable locking up capital for 3-7 years
- Illiquid allocation should be <10-15% of total portfolio

## Alternative Investments Overview

### Portfolio Satellite Allocation (10-20%)
Core portfolio (80-90%) should remain low-cost index funds. Satellite allocation can include:

| Investment | Key Vehicles | Role in Portfolio |
|-----------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Gold** | GLDM (0.10% ER), IAU | Crisis hedge, inflation protection. 5-10% allocation. Zero expected real return long-term. |
| **TIPS** | TIP, VTIP, SCHP | Inflation hedge. Best in tax-advantaged accounts (phantom income). |
| **Commodities** | PDBC, DJP | Broad commodity exposure. Inflation hedge. |

## Common Mistakes
- Underestimating maintenance costs (budget 1-2% of property value per year)
- Not accounting for vacancy (budget 5-10% of annual rent)
- Over-leveraging in rising rate environments
- Emotional buying (treating investment property like a personal home)
- Not running the numbers before buying (hope is not a strategy)
- Ignoring location fundamentals (job growth, population trends, schools)
- Being an accidental landlord (keeping a home as rental without planning)
